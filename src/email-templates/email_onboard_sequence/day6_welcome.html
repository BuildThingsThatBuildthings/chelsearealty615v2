Keep Your New Home Safe and Sound

Essential safety measures for your peace of mind


Welcome back to our home buying journey! In today's email, we're focusing on safety and emergency preparedness to ensure your new home remains a secure haven for you and your loved ones.

1. Installing and Maintaining Safety Equipment

Fire Extinguishers:

Choose ABC‑rated extinguishers for general use (and CO₂ types for kitchens or areas with electronics).
Install one in the kitchen, one in common areas, and consider additional units near bedrooms.
Check pressure gauges monthly and inspect for any damage or expiration dates.

Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Detectors:

Install smoke detectors in every bedroom, outside sleeping areas, and on every level.
Place CO detectors near sleeping areas and close to fuel‑burning appliances.
Test detectors monthly, replace batteries annually, and upgrade units every 7–10 years.

2. Building a Well‑Stocked First Aid Kit

Essential Supplies:

Include adhesive bandages, gauze, antiseptic wipes, and antibiotic ointment.
Stock burn cream, pain relievers, antihistamines, and personal medications.

Additional Tools:

Add scissors, tweezers, disposable gloves, and a digital thermometer.
Consider including a first aid manual for quick reference in emergencies.

3. Developing an Emergency Evacuation Plan

Plan Your Exits:

Draw a floor plan of your home and mark at least two exit routes from every room.
Designate a safe meeting point outside where everyone can gather.

Practice and Prepare:

Conduct evacuation drills with all household members at least twice a year.
Account for special needs, including children, pets, and mobility challenges.

4. Regular Safety Audits & Maintenance Schedules

Conduct Regular Inspections:

Monthly: Test smoke/CO detectors, check fire extinguishers, verify first aid kit.
Quarterly: Ensure exit routes are clear, review safety equipment condition.
Annually: Perform comprehensive audit and update emergency plan.

Keep a Safety Log:

Document inspection dates, findings, and corrective actions.
Set calendar reminders for routine maintenance tasks.

By proactively managing these safety measures, you can rest assured that your new home will be well-prepared to handle any unexpected situations. If you have any questions about setting up your safety equipment or developing an emergency plan, please feel free to reach out.
