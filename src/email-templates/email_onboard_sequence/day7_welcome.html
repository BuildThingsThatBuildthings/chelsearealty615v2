Long-Term Care: Home Maintenance Tips for Every Season

Keep your home in top condition year-round


Welcome back! Today we're shifting our focus to the ongoing care of your new home. Regular maintenance is key to preserving your investment and keeping your home running smoothly year-round.

1. Seasonal Maintenance Checklists

Spring:

Deep clean windows, screens, and outdoor furniture. Inspect roofs, gutters, and siding.
Power-wash siding and decks; trim trees and shrubs; reseal or repaint surfaces.
Service your air conditioning system and replace or clean filters.

Summer:

Clean or replace window screens; ensure proper attic ventilation.
Inspect exterior paint and repair any damage.
Maintain lawn with regular mowing and appropriate watering schedule.

Fall:

Clean gutters and downspouts; seal drafts around windows and doors.
Rake leaves, remove debris, and schedule HVAC maintenance.
Inspect roofing and insulate vulnerable areas.

Winter:

Inspect for ice dams, clear snow from pathways, ensure safe walkways.
Monitor heating systems and indoor humidity levels.
Insulate windows and doors; schedule professional inspections.

2. Routine Inspections

Monthly:

Test smoke and carbon monoxide detectors.
Inspect light fixtures, locks, and plumbing for issues.

Quarterly:

Clean refrigerator coils and inspect electrical components.
Check weatherstripping and seals around openings.

Annually:

Schedule professional HVAC, electrical, and plumbing inspections.
Perform deep cleaning and thorough roof inspection.

3. Organizing Your Maintenance Log

Document all routine tasks, seasonal projects, and inspection dates.
Use a spreadsheet or home maintenance app to track progress.
Schedule regular professional check-ups for major systems.

4. Outdoor Care & Landscaping Basics

Lawn Care:

Maintain regular mowing schedule with sharp blades.
Water early morning and follow local fertilization guidelines.

Garden & Landscape:

Regular weeding, pruning, and mulching for plant health.
Seasonal garden bed preparation and protection.

By following these maintenance guidelines, you can help ensure that your home remains in excellent condition for years to come. Regular care not only preserves your property's value but also creates a more comfortable and efficient living environment.
